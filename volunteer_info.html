Volunteer Information

Book mark this link for reference. I will probably update it a few times. When I do, I will let you know it changed!

Thanks for agreeing to volunteer.

Manning the registration desk

We will have a printed sheet with the attendee names on it. Registration desk volunteers ask arriving attendees for an ID and then verify that the person is on the list. If so, cross off the name, then hand the attendee a name card and lanyard. The name cards will be blank. We will have colored pens on hand for the attendees to use to write their name on the name card.

Mic and un-mic the speakers

We also need two volunteers to mic the speakers, one for each room. They must be mic'd completely a few minutes before their talk time.

We will use two lavalier mics per speaker. One mic is for our video production to capture the speaker's audio for our videos. Turn on the mics immediately after putting the on. The worst mic mistake is not having them on. Collect both mics right after each talk ends, and turn them off. Don't let them walk away with them!

That video production mic is connected to a pocket recorder. The mic goes on the shirt on the speaker in the upper middle of their chest (over their heart). It is connected via a wire to the pocket recorder. They literally put that in their pocket. The wire between the pocket recorder and the mic should go under the speakers shirt. The mics look like a small cylinder. The correct way to orient the cylinder is side to side, not up and down. They listen from both ends, left and right audio channels.

The other mic is from GMU for the room audio. The wire between the transmitter and the room mic should go under the speakers shirt. The transmitter clips to the speakers waistband or belt. GMU is supposed to have someone in each room that will put their room mics on the speakers (but not the video production mics), but they are unreliable sometimes, so our volunteer should take that job over if it is being missed.

Set up in the morning

We will have several boxes to bring up from the parking garage. We have a dolly to use, so we can bring up several at once. We will have table clothes for each table in both rooms (about 25 tables total). We need to put those on each table. There will be some food, silverware, etc. that needs to go on the serving tables. We might need to take a few boxes back to the truck in the garage at the end of the day.

Someone to help lay out the food and keep the tables clean

We will have breakfast, lunch and an afternoon snack. The food will be simple, like pizza, waters, coffee, etc. or in packaging the just needs to be opened, like cookies, or pastries. The food and drink will be delivered, or brought up in boxes from the garage. We will need help putting that out on the rectangular serving tables, along with napkins, plates, etc.

We will also have to remove obvious trash from the tables and throw it away near the end of the lunch break.

Introduce speakers in the Second Room (emcee)

The emcee introduces each speaker (just the facts is fine), handles the audience mic for questions, and keeps the speakers on schedule. Make sure to run the hand held mic to each question. That way, the videos will have the question in the audio feed (also it helps the audience hear the question). Sometimes getting them to end their talk requires getting their attention and signalling for them to wrap up.
