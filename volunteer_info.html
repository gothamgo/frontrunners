Manning the registration desk

We will have a printed sheet with the attendee names on it. Registration desk volunteers ask arriving attendees for an ID and then verify that the person is on the list. If so, cross off the name, then hand the attendee a name card and lanyard. The name cards will be blank. We will have colored pens on hand for the attendees to use to write their name on the name card.

Mic and un-mic the speakers

We also need two volunteers to mic the speakers. They must be mic'd completely at least 5 minutes before their talk. And the mics collected immediately after each talk ends.

We will use two lavalier mics per speaker. One mic is for our video production to capture the speaker's audio. That mic is connected to a pocket recorder. The mic goes on the shirt on the speaker near their face in the middle of their chest (ideally). The mics look like a small cylinder. The correct way to orient the cylinder is side to side, not up and down. They listen from both ends.

The other mic is from GMU and the other is for our video recording. GMU is supposed to have someone in each room that will put their mics on the speakers, but they are unreliable sometimes, so our volunteer should take that job over if it is being missed. Also, the volunteer should help put our mics on the speakers.
